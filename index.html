About me

Research at the intersection of cities, mobility, environment, and AI.

I am a Ph.D. student in Geospatial and Environmental Analysis at Virginia Tech. My work uses spatial analysis, computer vision, large language models, transit accessibility modeling, and human-centered data collection to understand how people experience urban environments.

I am especially interested in building transparent and useful geospatial tools that can support planning, transportation equity, and healthier public spaces.

Research themes

What I work on

GeoAI & multimodal models

Evaluating how vision-language models interpret streetscapes, spatial context, and built environment features.

Transportation accessibility

Measuring inequities in public transit access to essential services using GTFS, spatial statistics, and demographic data.

Human-centered sensing

Combining eye-tracking, computer vision, and field data to study active exposure to urban greenery and streetscape conditions.

Remote sensing & GIS

Using geospatial workflows, satellite imagery, and spatial modeling for environmental and urban research questions.
