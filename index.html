About me

Research at the intersection of cities, mobility, environment, and AI.

My work uses spatial analysis, computer vision, large language models, transit accessibility modeling, and field-based sensing to understand how people experience urban environments. I am especially interested in practical geospatial methods that can inform planning, transportation equity, public health, and environmental decision-making.

I have worked across GIS, remote sensing, photogrammetry, lidar-based movement analysis, accessibility modeling, and spatial database management in academic, public-facing, and applied geospatial settings.

Research themes

What I work on

My research combines computational geospatial methods with human-centered urban and environmental questions.

GeoAI & multimodal models

Evaluating how visual language models interpret streetscapes, spatial context, and built-environment conditions.

Transportation accessibility

Measuring inequities in public transit access to essential services using GTFS, R5R, spatial statistics, and demographic data.

Human-centered sensing

Combining eye-tracking, semantic segmentation, and field data to study active exposure to urban greenery.

Lidar & digital twins

Using terrestrial lidar to model indoor movement, occupancy patterns, object detection, and shared-space behavior.

Remote sensing & environmental GIS

Working with satellite imagery, Google Earth Engine, HPC workflows, and spatial overlay analysis for environmental questions.

UAV photogrammetry

Analyzing UAV imagery, GCP design, orthomosaics, DTM/DSM generation, and geospatial accuracy assessment.

Spatial databases & web GIS

Managing spatial data repositories, geoportals, PostGIS databases, and web mapping workflows.

AI in planning

Studying how planning institutions interpret, use, resist, and govern generative AI tools in public decision-making.
