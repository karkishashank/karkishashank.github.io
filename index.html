About me

Research at the intersection of cities, mobility, environment, and AI.

I am a Ph.D. student in Geospatial and Environmental Analysis at Virginia Tech. My work uses spatial analysis, computer vision, large language models, transit accessibility modeling, and field-based sensing to understand how people experience urban environments.

I am especially interested in practical geospatial methods that can inform planning, transportation equity, public health, environmental exposure assessment, and healthier public spaces.

Research themes

What I work on

My research combines computational geospatial methods with human-centered urban and environmental questions.

◎

GeoAI & multimodal models

Evaluating how visual language models interpret streetscapes, spatial context, and built-environment conditions.

⌁

Transportation accessibility

Measuring inequities in public transit access to essential services using GTFS, R5R, spatial statistics, and demographics.

◐

Human-centered sensing

Combining eye-tracking, semantic segmentation, and field data to study active exposure to urban greenery.

▧

Lidar & digital twins

Using terrestrial lidar to model indoor movement, occupancy patterns, object detection, and shared-space behavior.

◍

Remote sensing & environmental GIS

Working with satellite imagery, Google Earth Engine, HPC workflows, and spatial overlay analysis.

◇

UAV photogrammetry

Analyzing UAV imagery, GCP design, orthomosaics, DTM/DSM generation, and geospatial accuracy assessment.

▦

Spatial databases & web GIS

Managing spatial data repositories, geoportals, PostGIS databases, and web mapping workflows.

✦

AI in planning

Studying how planning institutions interpret, use, resist, and govern generative AI tools in decision-making.

Method pipeline

From spatial data to planning insight

01

Collect

Street-view images, GTFS, census data, eye-tracking, lidar, UAV, and satellite data.

02

Model

Spatial accessibility, computer vision, semantic segmentation, GeoAI, and statistical models.

03

Validate

Human ratings, scenario checks, accuracy assessment, and robustness testing.

04

Translate

Maps, dashboards, manuscripts, visual evidence, and planning-facing recommendations.

Selected work

Projects

GeoAI · Walkability

AI-Driven Walkability Assessment

Using street-view imagery, spatial context, human ratings, image augmentation, and multimodal LLMs to evaluate pedestrian environments and community-centered improvements.

Transit Equity

Transit Access to Health Care

Assessing public transit access to oral health and eye care providers in Virginia using GTFS, R5R, travel-time modeling, and regional equity analysis.

Eye-tracking · Computer Vision

Active Green Exposure

Quantifying human engagement with urban greenery using wearable eye-tracking, semantic segmentation, and walking/driving exposure comparisons.

Lidar · Digital Twins

Indoor Movement and Occupancy

Aggregating terrestrial lidar sensor data to detect movement, occupancy, and interactions in shared indoor spaces.

Planning · AI Governance

Generative AI in Urban Planning

Studying how generative AI is used in urban and spatial planning, including methods, data practices, spatial reasoning, and governance.
